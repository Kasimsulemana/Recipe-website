INGREDIENTS

2 cups maize flour (corn flour)

4 cups water

Salt (optional, to taste)

Ayoyo leaves (for the soup, optional)

Instructions

  1. Boil 3 cups of water in a pot over medium heat.
  2. In a bowl, mix 1 cup of maize flour with 1 cup of water to form a smooth, lump-free paste.
  3. Gradually pour the paste into the boiling water while stirring continuously.
  4. Allow the mixture to cook for a few minutes, then gradually add the remaining maize flour while stirring until it thickens.
  5. Keep stirring until the mixture forms a smooth and stretchy dough-like texture (this is the Tuo Zaafi).
  6. Serve hot with ayoyo soup, groundnut soup, or any preferred side dish.

Why I Chose This Recipe

I chose Tuo Zaafi because it is a cherished dish in the northern part of Ghana. It is not only nourishing but also a symbol of cultural pride. Tuo Zaafi is enjoyed with a variety of delicious soups, making it a beloved staple in many households.
